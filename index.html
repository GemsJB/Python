Theory Examination Guidelines
  • Written examination sat in Week 14 covering all theoretical content from Weeks 1–12
  • Comprises short-answer, multiple-choice, and structured questions mapped to Bloom's levels
  • Constitutes 60% of the final course grade
  • Students must score a minimum of 40% in the theory exam to pass the course
  • Open to resit once per academic year in accordance with KCA University regulations
ACM Computing Curricula 2023
PL/OOP, PL/Scripting, SE1 (software design). Weeks 10–12 map directly to ACM object-oriented programming competencies; Week 14 addresses software engineering life-cycle outcomes.
IEEE SWEBOK v4
Software construction, testing, and maintenance knowledge areas addressed in Weeks 9, 13–14. Error handling and debugging align with SWEBOK quality assurance practices.
Python Institute PCEP / PCAP
Core topics mapped: data types (Wk 3), control flow (Wks 5–6), functions (Wk 7), OOP (Wks 10–12), exception handling (Wk 13), and file I/O (Wk 9).
Bloom's Revised Taxonomy
Cognitive levels escalate: Remember/Understand (Wks 1–4) → Apply (Wks 5–9) → Analyze (Wks 8, 11–12) → Evaluate (Wk 12) → Create (Wk 14).
ABET Student Outcomes (CS)
SO1 (algorithms/structures), SO2 (design & development), SO4 (teamwork), SO6 (professional ethics) addressed across theory, lab, and mini-project components.
UNESCO ICT Competency Framework
Computational thinking (Wks 4–6, 8), collaborative creation (Wk 14 peer review), and professional communication align with UNESCO digital literacy pillars.
